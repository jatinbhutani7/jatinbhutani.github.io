Decision Layer

Translating observed patterns into actionable T20 decision principles:
1. Create a Control Phase Early
If the game never tightens, you are relying on errors. Powerplay or early middle overs must establish control.

2. Don’t Just Take Wickets — Build Pressure Around Them
Wickets without control overs release pressure instead of increasing it.

3. Convert Advantage Immediately
Strong starts or rebuilds only matter if followed by acceleration or squeeze. Delay loses control.

4. Identify and Attack the Weak Phase
Every innings has a soft phase (e.g., spin overs, fifth bowler). Winning teams target that phase decisively.

5. One Batter Must Control the Chase
In chases, at least one batter must dictate tempo. Without that, momentum becomes fragile.

6. Adapt Within the First 2–3 Overs of Failure
If Plan A is not working, delay in adjustment compounds damage. Tactical inertia loses games.
