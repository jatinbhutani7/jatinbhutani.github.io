01 System Summary
This system evaluates T20 matches through phase control and decision pressure. Early observations suggest that powerplay dominance and distributed batting structure significantly influence match outcomes. The core thesis: games are not won by accumulating advantages — they are won by converting them into control phases that the opposition cannot escape.

04 Decision Layer
01
Create a Control Phase Early
If the game never tightens, you are relying on errors. Powerplay or early middle overs must establish control.
02
Don't Just Take Wickets — Build Pressure Around Them
Wickets without control overs release pressure instead of increasing it.
03
Convert Advantage Immediately
Strong starts or rebuilds only matter if followed by acceleration or squeeze. Delay loses control.
04
Identify and Attack the Weak Phase
Every innings has a soft phase — spin overs, fifth bowler. Winning teams target that phase decisively.
05
One Batter Must Control the Chase
In chases, at least one batter must dictate tempo. Without that, momentum becomes fragile.
06
Adapt Within the First 2–3 Overs of Failure
If Plan A is not working, delay in adjustment compounds damage. Tactical inertia loses games.
