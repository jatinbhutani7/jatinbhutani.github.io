05Decision Insight
Core analytical finding
KKR did everything right for 15 overs and still lost. Death overs don't just add runs — they redefine the game's identity. The last phase has disproportionate leverage over all prior phases. KKR's two phases of control were made meaningless by one phase they couldn't hold.
06Framework Insights
The death phase has disproportionate leverage
SRH losing two phases and winning the match proves it: the last phase can negate all prior work. KKR's 15 overs of control counted for nothing once the death total was reset.
Concentrated phase plans can win matches
SRH didn't try to compete in every phase — they accepted losses in two and built their entire plan around one. Phase specialisation is a legitimate match strategy.
Chase plans must account for total flexibility
KKR's chase was calibrated for the score at over 16, not over 20. Structural rigidity in planning — no capacity to absorb a late total reset.
Two phases of dominance is insufficient without the third
KKR did the hard work for 15 overs. Without a death plan that matched SRH's, those phases became sunk cost, not foundation.
