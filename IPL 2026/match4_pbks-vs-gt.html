PBKS vs GT – Match Analysis

Turning Points

  • Chahal’s middle overs spell: Shifted GT from a platform (54/1) to a capped innings. Gill’s wicket came out of pressure, not a mistake.
  • Buttler’s stalled innings: Dot balls built pressure. He never got ahead of the game, and the dismissal followed.
  • PBKS collapse (118/2 → 118/5): Brought GT back into the game. Only phase where PBKS lost control.

Phase Control

Powerplay:
GT: Solid start, slightly ahead.
PBKS: Matched scoring in the chase.
→ Slight GT edge, not decisive.

Middle Overs:
GT: Lost control. Couldn’t rotate or accelerate. Chahal dictated tempo.
PBKS: Managed chase well before collapse.
→ PBKS ahead through control of tempo.

Death Overs:
GT: Took wickets but lacked control.
PBKS: Regained composure through Connolly and finished clearly.
→ PBKS controlled the decisive phase.

Net: GT had early control; PBKS had late control.

Match Decider

GT’s middle overs slowdown
They had the platform but no one took ownership after Gill. Dot balls increased, and the total stayed 15–20 runs short of pressure.

Decision Insight

PBKS trusted spin early and stayed with it. Chahal was introduced at the right time and used to create pressure rather than chase wickets. GT reacted late and were already behind when they tried to accelerate.

Win Probability Shift (directional)

  • 54/1: GT slightly ahead
  • Chahal phase + Gill wicket: game evens out
  • Buttler slowdown: PBKS edge
  • PBKS collapse (118/5): GT back ahead
  • Final overs: PBKS close it

Key point: GT had control early; PBKS had control late. Late control decided the game.

Framework Insights

  • Starts need a middle overs driver: GT built a base but never converted.
  • Dot balls create wickets: Pressure builds before dismissal.
  • One calm batter can stabilize a chase: Connolly held the innings together.
  • Wickets alone aren’t enough at the death: Control still matters.
