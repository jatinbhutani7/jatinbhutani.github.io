04Match Decider
The moment that made the game
KKR's Middle Overs Without a Trigger
The match was decided not by a moment, but by an absence. KKR's middle-overs slowdown had no tactical cause — no wickets, no bowling variation, no field pressure. When teams stall in the middle without external forcing, they hand the game without being beaten.
05Decision Insight
Core analytical finding
KKR had the powerplay but couldn't convert into sustained control. A middle-overs stall without wickets as the trigger is a self-inflicted control loss. The dangerous pattern isn't being outplayed — it's drifting when no one is forcing you to.
06Framework Insights
Middle overs without intent is the most dangerous phase
Teams that stall in the middle without tactical reasons are giving the opposition control for free. Boundaries don't need to come every over — but intent must.
Powerplay control must compound, not conclude
KKR won the powerplay and gained nothing from it. Platforms matter only if they create scoring acceleration or decision pressure in subsequent phases.
Wicket-free drift cannot be corrected late
Once the middle overs drift, the total is already set below par. Death overs acceleration cannot make up a full-phase concession.
Chase without pressure phases is controlled execution
MI never had to find a gear shift. When opposition totals are under-par, chasing becomes competence display rather than pressure management.
